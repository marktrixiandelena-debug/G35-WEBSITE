## 📝 Speaker Preparation & Delivery Checklist

To make sure the presentation flows naturally and confidently, run through this checklist before the big day:

### 1. Shaping the Presentation
- [ ] **The "Hook":** Do we have a strong opening statement that grabs the audience's attention and clearly sets our objectives?
- [ ] **Roadmap:** Have we smoothly outlined what each person will cover so the audience knows what to expect?
- [ ] **Logical Flow:** Do the transitions between presenters feel seamless (e.g., smoothly handing off from the problem to the solution)?
- [ ] **Visual Support:** Are the slides only used to support our main points, rather than acting as a script to read from?
- [ ] **The Wrap-Up:** Does our conclusion confidently summarize what we've built? Does it tie nicely back to the problems with Barangay Calauag we introduced in the beginning?

### 2. Mastering the Delivery
- [ ] **Own the Material:** Are all presenters knowledgeable and comfortable discussing their section without reading everything verbatim?
- [ ] **Organized Notes:** Are our speaking cues, index cards, or speaker notes organized and easy to glance at?
- [ ] **Room Recon:** Have we visited the presentation site to see where we will be standing, sitting, or presenting from?
- [ ] **Tech Check:** Have we tested our slides, Figma prototypes, and visual aids to ensure they are working and we know how to use them?

### 3. Professional Appearance & Body Language
- [ ] **Dress for Success:** Are we dressed and groomed appropriately, keeping the audience's professional expectations in mind?
- [ ] **Stage Presence:** Have we practiced presenting out loud while standing up (or sitting if applicable)? Are we maintaining good posture and welcoming body language?

### 4. Maximizing Visual Aids
- [ ] **Readability:** Are the fonts on our ERD and Figma screens large enough to be easily seen from all areas of the room?
- [ ] **Relevance:** Does every visual aid tie directly into the points we are trying to communicate?
- [ ] **Simplicity:** Are the slides easy to understand at a glance so the audience can focus on what we are saying instead of reading?
